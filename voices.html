Talking trash



Advocates on either side of the roll cart issue have been outspoken about the opinions. Former City Manager John Glascock once told the Missourian, "...If you really want to see people get upset, just mess with their trash." The Columbia Missourian has covered the issue of Columbia's trash system for years, talking to people on both sides of the debate about why they do or do not want roll carts, and how that impacts their lives. Hear some of the major arguments throughout the years cited in previous Missourian reporting.



"When we came here, we were kind of like ‘Well, where’s the garbage can?’"

-Rachel Proffitt, title, 2022, Trash collection changes are hot topic for candidates



"It’s a simple and elegant system. (The trash bag's) beautiful tuxedo black. You put it out there, and it’s gone."

-Richard Shanker, Solid Waste Advocacy Group, 2016, Ballot issue on roll carts approaches: How did we get here?



"The machine just picks it up, compared to those workers having (to collect). There’s just a lot of benefits that we will have from the roll cart system."

-Monica Lee, title, 2022, Trash collection changes are hot topic for candidates



"I think (roll carts) would be like throwing a wrench into what people are used to and their daily routine."

-Kathryn Leeper, former resident and MU student, 2016, Ballot issue on roll carts approaches: How did we get here?



"My motivation for roll carts has always been worker safety, first and foremost. When diving into the process and talking with city leaders, I was able to see the dire financial situation the solid waste utility is in. Now, my motivation is still worker safety but also includes maintaining our trash service as a city entity."

-Amy Belcher, title, 2020, Columbia group begins initiative petition to put trash roll carts on ballot



"Those of us who are devoted to the black-bag system like the fact that, No. 1, they don’t take up a lot of space and, No. 2, once they’re picked up off the street, the streets are clean."

-Chuck Headley, Solid Waste Advocacy Group, 2016, Ballot issue on roll carts approaches: How did we get here?
