The Columbia Trash System


The idea of roll carts are new to Columbia, but cities and towns across the nation use them regularly.
Take a look at the trash systems in Columbia and beyond.

Pay-as-you-throw system

For curbside trash to be picked up by the City of Columbia, it must be in the trash bags provided by the City.
Residents can redeem vouchers at select locations around Columbia. There is a limit of 104 bags per year, or 2 per week. Residents can purchase more bags if needed.



TRASHY: A roll of the City-official trash bags lies on a counter. To participate in curbside trash pickup, all trash must be in these City-approved bags. Photo by Rochita Ghosh


Curbside pickup

If Columbia residents have large items they wish to dispose of, they must complete a request form online and pay extra for the service.



CURB YOUR ENTHUSIASM: A residential Columbia house sits, covered largely by trees and bushes. In the house's neighborhood, the City picks up trash left on the curb on Tuesdays. Photo by Atreyo Ghosh, used with permission

Dumpsters

For residents who do not receive curbside collection -- typically those living in apartment complexes -- there are usually dumpsters nearby. These do not require the City-provided trash bags.

DUMPSTER DIVING: A dumpster sits in The Quarters parking lot. Residents take out their trash to one of the few dumpsters scattered throughout the complex. Photo by Rochita Ghosh


Roll carts

In other places, people place their trash bags into roll carts they leave at the curb.
